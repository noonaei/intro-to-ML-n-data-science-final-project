This project analyzes the relationship between social behavior and perceived loneliness using the ISSP 2017 Social Networks dataset.

We performed exploratory data analysis across demographic groups (country, age, and gender), applied correlation analysis and regression models, and engineered composite metrics for loneliness, family connections, and social activity.

Our findings suggest that the frequency of communication with friends or family is not strongly correlated with perceived loneliness, highlighting the complexity and subjectivity of social well-being.
